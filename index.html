Demographic Info

The first chart you see, the one on the top left measures the top 10 bacteria cultures per person. Of course, there are typically more than 10 cultures per person, this chart simply looks at the top 10 and displays the percentage of each one within the top-ten subgroup.

The chart on the top right measures the belly button washing frequency. Pretty gross, I get it, but important for the study.

The third chart, the one titled 'Cultures per Sample' shows a more complete picture of the first chart. With the third one we show every bacteria culture found and the size of the circle is proportional to the amount of that bacteria found.

The fourth chart, the one on the bottom left is simply another way of showing the first chart. Instead of showing the cultures as a bar, they are shown as parts of whole. Considering this study looks at individual people, a modified pie chart (a donut chart) is more apt for the occasion since they better convey information that is part of a discrete unit.
